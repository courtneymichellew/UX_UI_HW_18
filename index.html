Hi, I'm Courtney Michelle

A UX/UI Designer from Atlanta with a 10 year background in creating intuitive solutions for customers and users in the service industry. When designing, I aim to understand the major underlying problem and then create a tree of branched solutions that can be seamlessly applied to the customer journey ribbon. I am skilled in using design tools like, Figma, Miro, GitHub and Adobe Creative Suite.

My Skills

UI DESIGNING & BRANDING

My skills as a graphic designer in Brand communications have greatly improved since taking the UI portion of the UX/UI Bootcamp by the Georgia Institute of Technology.

PROTOTYPING

As a graphic designer, I am continuously going through the prototyping phase as I develop new designs for clients every day. After identifying and understanding their graphic communication needs, I create a draft (or prototype) of what the final product will look like. From here, the client and I test and improve the prototype until we land on a final product for distribution.

QUALITATIVE RESEARCH

During my time on a specialty team at Delta Air Lines, I was charged with improving the airport experience and service recovery experience through surveys, in-depth interviews, and observations. I gained valuable skills in synthesizing data and applying the results to creative initiatives for the benefit of the customer.

BSure

The BSure app helps users to plan their post-pandemic travel with greater peace of mind by allowing them to monitor the cases of COVID-19 in the countries they want to travel to as well as receive alerts when the numbers change for any destination.

JoyRide

The JoyRide app is for users in need of a safer way to rideshare. Its main feature is an SOS button that will allow users to communicate with police via text so as not to alert the driver. It also stores an emergency contact who is contacted by choice or when the SOS button has been activated.
